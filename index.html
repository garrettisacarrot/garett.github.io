BB-8

In my last year at Ithaca College I led a team of students in building a life sized working BB-8. I was awarded a James B. Pendleton Grant for student research. This project required a synthesis of many of my skills, like 3D printing, programming, and social media outreach. The parts are all 3D printed and the electronics and motor controls are all based on schematics we made specifically for this project. In April 2019, the year after my graduation, we were proud to present our work and its application to STEM education at Star Wars Celebration 2019. The Ithaca College newspaper even published a story about us.

Tanks the Constructible Card Game

As a class project to reskin an existing game for marketing purposes I chose to reskin the tabletop game Pirates the Constructable Card Game. The original game is a tabletop strategy game that once included content from Disney's Pirates of the Caribbean. Using the same concept I reskinned and redesigned the game to be played in VR using WWII era tanks. Using community sourced 3D models I created the game using the Tabletop Simulator platform and released it online. I also created my own cards to accompany the models using Adobe Photoshop, rewrote the rules document, all guided by a mood board.

Here is the mood board that inspired the style:

The game was heavily influenced by the coffee stained colors of old documents, especially those seen in movies. The style also draws inspiration from other contemporary WWII games such as World of Tanks.
